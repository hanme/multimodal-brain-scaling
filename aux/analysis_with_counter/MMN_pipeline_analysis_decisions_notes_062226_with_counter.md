## Decision 0 (new) — Interpreting counterbalanced results

The counterbalanced pairs provide a within-stimulus control for frequency-preference
artifacts. All analyses in this section use the **S2 criterion** (interior trough + 50%
recovery in 100–240 ms) rather than the magnitude-only C0. S2 is preferred here because
it requires a genuine dip-and-recover waveform shape, filtering out the ramp responses
that constitute most encoder C0 positives. The interpretation key is:

| Observed pattern (S2) | Interpretation |
| --------------------- | -------------- |
| Both regular AND counter S2-positive | Strong evidence for genuine deviance detection with a trough-shaped waveform in both directions |
| Regular-only S2-positive | Consistent with a frequency-preference or asymmetric baseline effect; the counter direction does not produce a qualifying trough |
| Counter-only S2-positive | The original regular result was either marginal or ramp-driven; the counter direction produces the more genuine response |
| Neither direction S2-positive | No shape-qualified MMN in either direction from this model × stimulus pair |

**Key findings (S2 criterion)** — see `results_analysis_with_counter.md` Section 0b for full tables:

- **mTRF is strongly bidirectional under S2**: 11–18/20 pairs per model show both-MMN
  (55–90% bidirectionality). The "both no-MMN" cell is zero or one for every model,
  meaning every S2-positive regular result is almost always matched by an S2-positive
  counter result. Whisper-small is the most symmetric (18/20 both-MMN, 2/20 disagree);
  whisper-base shows the most counter advantage (regular 12/20, counter 19/20 under S2),
  reflecting marginal regular stimuli gaining S2-qualifying troughs in the counter direction.

- **Encoder is dominated by "both no-MMN" under S2**: 9–15/20 pairs per model fall in the
  both-no-MMN cell, confirming that the encoder rarely produces genuine troughs in either
  direction. This is not a counterbalancing failure — it reflects the encoder's pre-existing
  shape deficit (S2/C0 retention ~26% in Section 4). The small residual disagreement
  (5–10/20) consists of borderline cases, not systematic directional biases.

- **Per-method (S2)**: mTRF per-method counts range 4–8/8 regular and 6–8/8 counter
  (Table CB-3a), confirming no method shows a systematic regular-only advantage. Methods 72
  and 75 (1000→1200 Hz) achieve 8/8 in both directions — the most robust bidirectional
  evidence. Method 60 (1000→1500 Hz, regular 4/8, counter 8/8) shows the largest gap.
